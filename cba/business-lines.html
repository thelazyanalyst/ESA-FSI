CBA — Business Lines & Processes

Research date: 2026-03-18

Retail Banking Services

Australia's largest retail banking operation.

Products & services:

  • Savings and transaction accounts
  • Home loans (including Unloan digital brand)
  • Personal loans and car loans
  • Credit cards
  • Buy Now Pay Later (StepPay)
  • Insurance: home, car, health, life, travel, pet
  • Foreign exchange
  • Digital banking via the CommBank app

Key processes:

  • Credit decisioning via ML models
  • Digital origination (Unloan — fully digital home loan)
  • Personalisation engine / Benefits Finder (AI-driven)
  • Anti-scam tools: NameCheck, CallerCheck
  • Customer onboarding via app (biometric ID verification)

Distribution:

  • Branch network (Australia-wide)
  • CommBank app (8M+ digitally active customers, #1 ranked in Australia)
  • Broker channel (home loans)
  • ATM network

Business Banking

Services SMEs and commercial customers.

Products & services:

  • Business transaction and savings accounts
  • Business lending (overdrafts, term loans, lines of credit)
  • Merchant services and payment terminals
  • Cash flow finance (invoice finance, trade finance)
  • Asset and equipment finance
  • Business insurance
  • FX and international payments

Key processes:

  • Relationship manager-led coverage for commercial clients
  • Digital business banking platform (NetBank Business)
  • Integrated merchant acquiring and banking

Institutional Banking & Markets (IB&M)

Services large corporations, government, and institutional investors.

Products & services:

  • Corporate finance and advisory
  • Debt capital markets (bonds, syndicated loans)
  • Transaction banking (cash management, payments, custody)
  • Risk management products (FX, interest rate derivatives)
  • Trade and supply chain finance
  • Equity capital markets access
  • Project finance

Key processes:

  • Deal origination via coverage teams
  • Markets trading and risk management
  • Settlement and custody operations
  • Correspondent banking and international payments

ASB Bank (New Zealand)

CBA's wholly owned NZ subsidiary. One of NZ's major banks.

Products & services:

  • Personal banking (home loans, deposits, credit cards, personal loans)
  • Business banking (SME to corporate)
  • Rural banking (agribusiness lending)
  • KiwiSaver (retirement savings)
  • Insurance products
  • Foreign exchange
  • Digital banking (ASB Mobile, FastNet Classic)

HQ: ASB North Wharf, 12 Jellicoe Street, Auckland CBD

Key processes:

  • NZ-specific credit and regulatory compliance (RBNZ, FMA)
  • Rural relationship banking
  • KiwiSaver fund management

x15ventures (Fintech Portfolio)

CBA's corporate venture and innovation arm.

Portfolio companies include:

  • Backr (business support platform for SMEs)
  • Vonto (data intelligence for small business)
  • Unloan (digital home loans)
  • Other early-stage fintech ventures

Purpose: Incubate and scale adjacent financial services businesses outside the core bank.

Technology & AI Strategy

Key platforms and initiatives:

  • CommBank app — ranked #1 in Australia; central to customer engagement
  • Azure OpenAI integration — GenAI tools for 5,000+ staff (coding, customer service scripting, document summarisation)
  • ML fraud models — NameCheck, CallerCheck (real-time payment fraud prevention)
  • Benefits Finder — AI tool matching customers to government entitlements
  • Multi-cloud architecture — AWS + Microsoft Azure
  • Cyber operations centre — dedicated SOC; ~300 additional cyber staff hired 2022–2024

Strategic posture:

  • "Technology company with a banking licence" (Matt Comyn, recurring)
  • AUD 2B+ annual technology investment
  • Cost-to-income target: below 42%
  • Cybersecurity cited as "top three" board-level risk

Executive Statements on AI & Cybersecurity

Matt Comyn (CEO):

  • AFR Banking Summit, March 2024: "AI is the most significant technology shift in banking since the internet"
  • Confirmed internal GenAI deployment (Azure OpenAI) to 5,000 staff, FY2024
  • Fraud prevention via AI: ~AUD 350M prevented in FY2024
  • Cybersecurity: "top three" board-level risk; ACSC Partnership Program member

Alan Docherty (CFO):

  • Technology investment described as a "strategic moat," protected from short-term cost reduction
  • Cost-to-income ratio target: below 42%

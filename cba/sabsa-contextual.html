Contextual Architecture — The Business View

The Contextual Architecture is the top layer of the SABSA framework. It captures the business context — who CBA is, what it is trying to achieve, what risks it faces, and the environmental constraints it operates within. Everything in the layers below must be traceable back to this layer. This document is derived from CBA's public annual results, CEO statements, investor presentations, and technology disclosures (2023–2026).

WHAT — Business Assets
Goals · Values · Asset Taxonomy · Success Factors · Targets
Business Goals & Decisions

Maintain and extend CBA's position as Australia's most profitable bank and most digitally advanced financial institution. Operate as a "technology company with a banking licence" — where technology investment is a strategic moat, not a cost to be minimised. Sustain above-peer cost efficiency (cost-to-income below 42%), grow business and home lending, and continue to lead in AI-driven fraud prevention and customer personalisation.

Business Values
  • "Technology company with a banking licence" — Matt Comyn's recurring strategic identity; technology is described as a "strategic moat" protected from cost-cutting
  • Customer protection as competitive differentiation — NameCheck, CallerCheck, and fraud AI not just compliance tools but brand trust assets; AUD 350M in fraud prevented FY24
  • AI leadership with responsibility — 2,000+ ML models, 55M AI decisions/day; investment in AI literacy (30,000+ staff trained; Future Workforce Program)
  • Financial inclusion — Benefits Finder AI matches customers to government entitlements; Unloan offers accessible digital home loans
  • Long-term investment discipline — AUD 8B+ in technology over five years; technology spend protected from short-term cost pressure by CFO commitment
Taxonomy of Business Assets
  • Customer relationships — 8M+ digitally active customers; CommBank app ranked #1 in Australia; 70%+ of transactions digital
  • Home loan book — ~A$574B home loans (Nov 2024); ~68% of total lending; #1 Australian home lender; Unloan digital brand for new origination
  • Business lending book — A$168B (Dec 2025, +12% half-on-half); Business Banking is 42% of Group cash NPAT in H1 FY26
  • CommBank app and digital infrastructure — #1 ranked banking app in Australia; central to customer engagement, fraud prevention, and cross-sell
  • AI and data platform — 2,000+ ML models trained on ~157B data points; 55M AI decisions/day; Evident AI Index global rank #4 (2025); Azure OpenAI integration for 5,000+ staff
  • ASB Bank (New Zealand) — wholly owned; NZD $1,350M NPAT FY25; NZD $18.8B KiwiSaver FUM; total capital 16.3%
  • x15ventures — fintech portfolio (Backr, Vonto, Unloan) for adjacent growth beyond core banking
  • Capital position — CET1 12.0% (Mar 2025); operates well above APRA "unquestionably strong" benchmark of 10.5%; AUD 2B+ annual technology investment
Critical Success Factors
  • Technology lead maintenance — AUD 2B+ annual investment must continue to widen the gap over peers; any slowdown risks losing the "technology moat" differentiation
  • AI fraud prevention effectiveness — 76% reduction in scam losses from peak; any material deterioration in fraud prevention outcomes is a direct brand and regulatory risk
  • NIM management — NIM of 2.04% (H1 FY26, down 4bps); home lending margin under competitive pressure; volume growth in business lending must compensate
  • Cost-to-income discipline — below 42% target; technology investment must generate measurable productivity returns to sustain this ratio
  • Cybersecurity posture — cited as "top three" board-level risk; cyber incidents on a bank of CBA's profile would be disproportionately reputational and financial
  • Regulatory compliance — APRA AT1 phase-out (Jan 2027); CDR/open banking obligations; AUSTRAC AML/CTF compliance
Business Targets
  • Profitability: Cash NPAT A$10.25B FY25; H1 FY26 A$5.45B (+7.4% vs H1 FY25); operating income +6.6% H1 FY26
  • Cost-to-income: below 42% (strategic target); pre-provision profit A$8.13B H1 FY26 (+5.3%)
  • Business lending: A$168B book (Dec 2025); +12% half-on-half growth; 42% of Group cash NPAT in H1 FY26
  • Home lending: A$574B (Nov 2024); +7% growth in H1 FY26; protect #1 market position
  • AI fraud: sustain 76% reduction in scam losses from peak; continue 20% half-on-half improvement trajectory
  • Dividend: A$2.35 per share interim (H1 FY26, +10c on prior); sustain fully franked dividend growth
  • Capital: CET1 ~12.0–12.5%; above APRA "unquestionably strong" 10.5% benchmark; manage AT1 phase-out from Jan 2027

WHY — Business Motivation
Risk · Opportunities & Threats · Risk Management Strategy
Opportunities & Threats
Opportunities
  • Business banking growth — A$168B book growing at +12%; margin 336bps; opportunity to extend #1 position further in SME and commercial
  • AI leadership compounds — 2,000+ models and 55M daily decisions create a data flywheel advantage that widens with scale; Evident AI #4 globally
  • x15ventures — optionality from fintech portfolio; Unloan and Backr can capture segments outside traditional banking without cannibalising core margins
  • Open banking / CDR — as largest bank, CBA can leverage data scale advantage in personalisation and product recommendations via Benefits Finder-style tools
  • ASB NZ growth — KiwiSaver FUM +12% (A$18.8B); NZ open banking tailwind; home lending +7%
Threats
  • NIM compression — NIM 2.04% (down 4bps H1 FY26); competitive home lending market and funding cost pressure continue to erode margin
  • Cybersecurity at scale — operating at #4 AI rank globally and 55M decisions/day makes CBA a high-value target; adversarial AI represents an escalating threat
  • Regulatory burden — APRA AT1 phase-out (Jan 2027) requires capital restructuring; CDR obligations ongoing; AUSTRAC AML/CTF compliance costs
  • Scam and fraud sophistication — despite 76% reduction from peak, AI-driven social engineering is evolving; Code of Banking Practice liability for APP fraud
  • Competition from non-banks and neobanks — Unloan itself signals that digital-only lenders can disrupt the incumbent home loan model
Risk Management Strategy
  • Technology as risk control — AUD 900M+ in AI/cyber/fraud investment (FY25); fraud prevention AI directly reduces financial and reputational risk
  • Dedicated cyber operations — dedicated SOC; ~300 additional cyber staff hired 2022–2024; cybersecurity rated "top three" board-level risk
  • ACSC partnership — member of Australian Cyber Security Centre partnership programme; early threat intelligence access
  • Capital buffer above regulatory minimum — CET1 well above 10.5% "unquestionably strong" threshold; provides buffer for unexpected losses
  • AI governance — 30,000+ staff AI-trained; Future Workforce Program ($90M); responsible AI framework governing 2,000+ models

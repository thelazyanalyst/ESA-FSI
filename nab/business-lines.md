# NAB (National Australia Bank) — Business Lines & Processes

**Research date:** 2026-03-18

---

## Personal Banking

Services Australian retail/consumer customers.

**Products & services:**
- Home loans (owner-occupier and investment; target: proprietary channel 41% of drawdowns)
- Savings and transaction accounts
- Term deposits
- Credit cards
- Personal and car loans
- ubank (digital-only bank subsidiary)
- Foreign exchange

**Key processes:**
- Broker and proprietary home lending channels
- ubank — passkeys deployed; passwords to be phased out within 5 years
- Digital origination and servicing
- Proprietary channel share grew from 38% (FY24) to 41% (FY25)

**FY25 performance:** Down 9.2% on prior period — pressured by home loan margin compression and competition

---

## Business & Private Banking (B&PB)

NAB's defining and dominant division. Australia's leading business bank.

**Products & services:**
- SME lending (NAB's core strength)
- Commercial and agribusiness lending
- Asset and equipment finance
- Health sector specialist banking
- Private banking for high-net-worth individuals
- Business transaction accounts and deposits
- Cash flow finance and trade finance
- NAB Connect (online business banking platform)

**Key processes:**
- Relationship manager-led coverage
- Specialist sector teams (agri, health, professional services, First Nations)
- Banker chat assistant: GenAI scans and summarises trust deeds — review time cut from 45 min to 5 min, saving ~10,000 hours/year
- "Customer brain" — centralised customer intelligence layer across all touchpoints

**FY25 performance:** A$1.74B cash earnings (half-year); business lending +9% FY25; deposits +7.4%
**CEO priority:** "Grow the business bank" — extend NAB's position as Australia's leading business bank

**CEO pledges:**
- $60B in housing finance by 2030 (~55,000 first home buyers, 50,000 new homes)
- $1B in First Nations business lending by end 2026
- $128B in new business lending supported in 2025

---

## Corporate & Institutional Banking (CIB)

Services large corporations, government, and institutional clients.

**Products & services:**
- Corporate finance and advisory
- Debt capital markets (syndicated loans, bonds)
- Transaction banking (cash management, trade finance, payments)
- Foreign exchange and interest rate risk management
- Structured finance and leveraged buyout finance
- Infrastructure and project finance

**FY25 performance:** Down 7.6% (1H25) — pressured by margin compression and lower fee income

---

## Bank of New Zealand (BNZ)

NAB's wholly-owned NZ subsidiary. Financial year ends 30 September.

**Three reportable segments:**
1. **Private, Wealth & Insurance** — high-net-worth banking, investments, insurance (wealth business was sold, restructuring reflected in recent periods)
2. **Corporate & Institutional Banking** — large NZ corporates, government, institutional
3. **BNZ Partnership Banking** — retail and SME customers (equivalent of personal + business banking for NZ)

**Products & services:**
- Personal banking (home loans, deposits, credit cards, KiwiSaver)
- Business banking (SME to corporate)
- Agribusiness lending
- Insurance
- Institutional banking

**FY2025:** Net profit NZD $1,499M (flat on FY2024); lending +4.6%; deposits +5.8%

## Cybersecurity Strategy

**"Secure by Design"** — NAB's five-pillar security framework (led by CSO Sandro Bucchianeri):

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| Protect | Threat and data-led protection approach |
| Security culture and mindset | Enterprise-wide security awareness |
| Simplification and automation | Reduce complexity; automate repetitive security tasks |
| Layered security | Match tools and skills to understood threats |
| Secure by design | "Shift left" — security baked into development from the start |

**Fusion Centre:** Physical security, cybersecurity, and global operations combined for faster incident response.

**Key partnerships and tools:**
- CrowdStrike — enterprise endpoint security platform; extended free to SMB banking customers (12 months) as a differentiator
- Microsoft — cyber assessments for SMB customers
- AWS — multi-cloud infrastructure with security controls
- ACSC partnership and Australian Government Cyber Security Strategy alignment

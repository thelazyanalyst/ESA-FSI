# Westpac Banking Corporation — Business Lines & Processes

**Research date:** 2026-03-18

---

## Consumer Bank

Services ~9 million Australian consumer customers across multiple brands.

**Brands:** Westpac, St George, BankSA, Bank of Melbourne, RAMS

**Products & services:**
- Savings and transaction accounts
- Home loans (owner-occupier and investment)
- Personal loans
- Credit cards
- Foreign exchange and travel banking
- Insurance (home, car, life)
- Online and mobile banking

**Key processes:**
- Multi-brand retail strategy across Australian states
- Digital onboarding and servicing via Westpac app and online banking
- Mortgage origination via branch, broker, and digital channels

**FY2025:** Net profit A$2.9B (+4% on FY2024)

---

## Business & Wealth

Combines commercial/business banking and BT wealth management. Largest profit contributor in 1H25.

**Business banking products:**
- SME and commercial lending
- Agribusiness banking
- Cash flow finance and trade finance
- Asset and equipment finance
- Business transaction banking and treasury services
- Merchant services and payments

**Wealth products (BT Financial Group):**
- Superannuation (BT Super)
- Managed funds and investment platforms (Panorama, BT Wrap)
- Wealth advisory services

**Key processes:**
- Relationship manager-led commercial banking
- BT Panorama: digital investment platform for advisers and self-directed investors
- UNITE transformation driving operational efficiency in this division

**FY2025:** Net profit A$2.2B (-7%, UNITE investment drag)
**1H25:** Net profit A$1.14B (+20%) — became largest contributor for the half

---

## Westpac Institutional Bank (WIB)

Services corporations, government entities, and institutional investors in Australia, NZ, and internationally.

**Products & services:**
- Financial and debt capital markets
- Corporate and structured finance
- Trade and supply chain finance
- Foreign exchange and interest rate risk management
- Transaction banking (cash management, payments)
- Project and infrastructure finance

**International presence:** UK, Singapore, Hong Kong, Tokyo, Beijing, Shanghai, Mumbai, Jakarta, New York, Fiji, Papua New Guinea

**FY2025:** Net profit A$1.6B (+15%) — strongest growth of any division

---

## Westpac New Zealand

Full-service banking for NZ consumers, businesses, and institutions.

**Products & services:**
- Retail banking (home loans, deposits, credit cards)
- Business banking (SME to corporate)
- Rural/agribusiness lending
- Insurance products
- Institutional banking

**FY2025 highlights:**
- Net profit: NZD ~$1,197M (up 13%)
- Home lending: NZD $71.3B (up 5%)
- Business lending: NZD $34.2B (up 2%)
- Household deposits: ~NZD $81B (up 6%)

## Technology & AI Strategy — UNITE Transformation

**UNITE** is Westpac's multi-year, multi-billion-dollar technology simplification and transformation program.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Total UNITE investment | ~A$2B+ |
| FY25 actual UNITE spend | A$660M |
| FY26 guided UNITE spend | A$850–950M |
| Annual savings target (by 2030) | A$750M |
| Full benefit accrual | Extended to FY29 |
| Initiatives (as at March 2025 update) | 60 consolidated; 41 in progress; 4 completed |

**AI and digital initiatives:**

| Initiative | Description |
|---|---|
| Intelligence Layer | Enterprise-wide centralised AI infrastructure — enables data-driven decisions across all business units |
| AI call assistant | Real-time AI analysis of customer phone calls in fraud/scam specialist teams — flags scam coaching indicators live |
| Conversational AI (Kasisto) | "Central conversational AI brain" for banking interactions |
| SafeBlock / SafeCall | AI-driven fraud prevention tools; A$400M+ in customer losses prevented since 2023 |
| Dr Andrew McMullan (Chief Data, Digital & AI Officer) | Recruited from CBA (Sep 2025); mandate to accelerate AI adoption bank-wide |

**Responsible AI:** Eight published Responsible AI Principles governing design, delivery, deployment, and use of AI systems.

## Executive Statements

**Anthony Miller (CEO, since December 2024):**

- **Strategy:** "Stay the course" — no radical pivot from predecessor's strategy; continued UNITE execution
- **Regional banking reset:** "We haven't always got our regional banking strategy right in the past" — trialling banker services in communities without major bank branches; rolling out to all states through 2026
- **Capital:** "We've managed margins in a competitive environment and our capital position is strong, providing us with plenty of flexibility"
- **Hiring:** 350 additional customer-facing bankers added
- **Intelligence Layer:** Cited as example of "enterprise-wide mindset" on AI adoption
- **AI call assistant:** "A game-changer" for fraud/scam detection on live customer calls
- **March 2026:** Commented on energy price spikes adding to RBA's challenge on rates/macro
- **Community investment:** Reorienting toward education

**Westpac cyber posture:**
- A$400M+ in customer losses prevented since 2023 via SafeBlock, SafeCall, and AI tools
- NZ fraud prevention up 11% (early 2025)
- 76% decrease in customer scam losses from peak (H2 FY23 vs H2 FY25)
- APRA capital overlay removal (October 2025) confirmed completion of multi-year risk transformation

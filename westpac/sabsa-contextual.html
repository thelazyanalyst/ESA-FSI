Contextual Architecture — The Business View

The Contextual Architecture is the top layer of the SABSA framework. It captures the business context — who Westpac is, what it is trying to achieve, what risks it faces, and the environmental constraints it operates within. Everything in the layers below must be traceable back to this layer. This document is derived from Westpac's public annual results, CEO statements, UNITE programme updates, and regulatory filings (2023–2026).

WHAT — Business Assets
Goals · Values · Asset Taxonomy · Success Factors · Targets
Business Goals & Decisions

Complete the UNITE technology simplification programme and realise A$750M in annual savings by 2030. "Stay the course" — CEO Anthony Miller has signalled continuity rather than a radical strategic pivot from his predecessor. Strengthen the multi-brand consumer banking model, grow Business & Wealth, sustain WIB momentum, and expand regional banking services. Build an enterprise-wide AI capability via the Intelligence Layer and clean the regulatory slate (APRA capital overlay fully removed October 2025).

Business Values
  • Responsible AI — eight published Responsible AI Principles governing design, delivery, deployment, and use of AI systems; AI governance as a board-level commitment
  • Community and regional banking — "We haven't always got our regional banking strategy right in the past" (CEO Miller); trialling banker services in communities without major bank branches; reorienting toward education investment
  • Customer protection — SafeBlock/SafeCall: A$400M+ in customer losses prevented since 2023; 76% decrease in customer scam losses from peak (H2 FY23 → H2 FY25)
  • Accountability and transparency — AUSTRAC A$1.3B penalty (2020) followed by sustained multi-year risk transformation; APRA capital overlay fully removed October 2025 as proof of completion
  • Enterprise-wide AI mindset — Intelligence Layer described by CEO Miller as an example of the "enterprise-wide mindset" required for AI adoption; not siloed by business unit
Taxonomy of Business Assets
  • Multi-brand consumer franchise — Westpac, St George, BankSA, Bank of Melbourne, RAMS; ~9M Australian consumer customers; geographic and demographic diversification through brand strategy
  • Business & Wealth division — BT Financial Group (Panorama, BT Wrap) + commercial banking; A$2.2B net profit FY25; largest profit contributor in 1H25
  • Westpac Institutional Bank (WIB) — strongest growth division; A$1.6B net profit FY25 (+15%); international presence (UK, Singapore, HK, Tokyo, Beijing, Shanghai, Mumbai, Jakarta, NY, Fiji, PNG)
  • Westpac New Zealand — NZD ~$1,197M net profit FY25 (+13%); home lending NZD $71.3B; business lending NZD $34.2B
  • UNITE platform — ~A$2B+ investment; 41 initiatives in progress; Intelligence Layer (enterprise AI); A$750M annual savings target by 2030
  • Intelligence Layer — enterprise-wide centralised AI infrastructure; enables data-driven decisions across all business units; AI call assistant for fraud/scam detection on live calls
  • SafeBlock / SafeCall — A$400M+ in customer losses prevented since 2023; 76% reduction in scam losses from peak; NZ fraud prevention up 11% (early 2025)
  • Capital position — CET1 12.5% (FY25); APRA A$1.0B capital overlay fully removed October 2025; gross loans A$856B (+6% YoY)
Critical Success Factors
  • UNITE programme delivery — A$660M invested FY25; A$850–950M FY26; full benefit accrual extended to FY29; programme execution is the single biggest operational risk and opportunity
  • Intelligence Layer adoption — enterprise AI capability is only valuable if adopted consistently across all business units; CEO Miller has named it as the AI model for the whole bank
  • APRA relationship normalisation — capital overlay removal (Oct 2025) confirms risk transformation completion; maintaining this standing is essential for capital flexibility
  • Consumer bank NIM management — NIM 1.94% (down 1bp FY25); multi-brand strategy must drive sufficient volume to compensate for margin pressure
  • WIB momentum — +15% FY25 is the strongest divisional performance; international institutional franchise must be sustained through any macro or geopolitical headwinds
  • Regional banking credibility — CEO Miller's regional banking reset (350 additional customer-facing bankers; all states through 2026) must demonstrate tangible outcomes to justify the cost
Business Targets
  • UNITE savings: A$750M annual savings target by 2030; FY26 UNITE investment A$850–950M; full benefit by FY29
  • Profitability: Statutory net profit FY25 A$6.9B; net profit excl. Notable Items A$7.0B; ROTE 11.1% (excl. Notable Items)
  • Gross loans: A$856B FY25 (+6% YoY); sustain growth across Consumer, Business, WIB, and NZ
  • NIM: 1.94% (FY25, down 1bp); defend through volume growth and product mix management
  • Capital: CET1 12.5% FY25; maintain above APRA "unquestionably strong" 10.5%; LCR >100%
  • Customer scam protection: sustain A$400M+ in annual fraud losses prevented; continue 76% reduction trajectory from peak
  • Regional banking: 350 additional bankers added; expand to all states through 2026

WHY — Business Motivation
Risk · Opportunities & Threats · Risk Management Strategy
Opportunities & Threats
Opportunities
  • APRA overlay removal (Oct 2025) — A$1.0B capital overhead eliminated; frees capital for growth, dividend, or buyback; marks end of the post-AUSTRAC regulatory penalty era
  • UNITE savings realisation — A$750M annual savings by 2030 creates significant profit leverage if programme delivers on schedule
  • Intelligence Layer compounding — enterprise-wide AI platform creates a scalable data advantage; AI call assistant for fraud/scam detection is "a game-changer" (CEO Miller)
  • WIB international momentum — +15% FY25; Asia-Pacific trade and capital flows provide structural tailwind for institutional business
  • Regional banking reset — underbanked communities create a genuine growth opportunity if Westpac can execute community banker model effectively across all states
Threats
  • UNITE execution risk — programme extended to FY29 (from FY28); any further delay increases investment cost and postpones savings; A$660M already spent in FY25 alone
  • AUSTRAC compliance legacy — A$1.3B penalty (2020) required sustained multi-year remediation; re-emergence of AML/CTF compliance failures would be catastrophic
  • NIM compression — 1.94% (down 1bp); consumer bank home lending margins under sustained competitive pressure; multi-brand strategy adds complexity without always adding margin
  • Energy and macro volatility — CEO Miller (March 2026) specifically cited energy price spikes as complicating RBA's interest rate decisions; macro uncertainty affects credit demand
  • APRA RWA overlay risk — A$1B overlay from December 2024 (customer risk grade proxy issue on non-retail exposures subset) pending rectification; any failure to rectify could reimpose capital drag
  • AI adversarial risk at scale — AI call assistant and Intelligence Layer are high-value targets for adversarial manipulation; responsible AI principles must be backed by robust controls
Risk Management Strategy
  • Eight Responsible AI Principles — published framework covering design, delivery, deployment, and use; positions Westpac as a leader in responsible AI governance among Australian banks
  • SafeBlock / SafeCall — A$400M+ in customer losses prevented since 2023; real-time AI fraud detection on live customer phone calls via AI call assistant; NZ fraud up 11% (early 2025)
  • AUSTRAC remediation completion — multi-year risk transformation programme completed October 2025 (APRA overlay fully removed); sustained investment in AML/CTF controls
  • Intelligence Layer governance — enterprise-wide AI infrastructure controlled centrally; avoids ungoverned shadow AI proliferation across business units
  • Capital management above benchmarks — CET1 12.5%; LCR 131%; well above APRA minimums; capital flexibility restored post-overlay removal
  • Dr Andrew McMullan (Chief Data, Digital & AI Officer) — recruited from CBA (September 2025); mandate to accelerate AI adoption with governance; CBA pedigree brings proven responsible AI experience

# ANZ Bank — Business Lines & Processes

**Research date:** 2026-03-18

---

## Australia Retail

Services Australian consumer customers.

**Products & services:**
- Home loans (including variable, fixed, offset)
- Savings and transaction accounts
- Term deposits
- Credit cards
- Personal loans
- Foreign exchange
- Insurance products
- Digital banking (ANZ Plus app, ANZ Internet Banking)

**Key processes:**
- Mortgage origination via branch, broker, and digital channels
- Suncorp Bank integration (customer migration underway; target June 2027)
- ANZ Plus — next-generation digital banking platform
- Retail credit decisioning

**Key challenge:** Underperforming in FY24–FY25 due to home loan margin compression and competition from non-bank lenders and digital challengers.

---

## Business & Private Bank

Services SMEs, mid-market commercial, agribusiness, and high-net-worth private banking clients.

**Products & services:**
- Business lending (overdrafts, term loans, asset finance)
- Business transaction and savings accounts
- Agribusiness lending
- Private banking for high-net-worth individuals
- Commercial real estate finance
- Equipment and asset finance
- Specialist sector banking (health, professional services, etc.)

**Key processes:**
- Relationship manager coverage model
- Salesforce Agentforce AI: deployed at scale (first in Asia-Pacific) to surface leads, summarise customer data, and prioritise daily banker activities — saves ~1 working month/year per banker
- SME digital banking platform

---

## Institutional

Services global institutional and corporate clients. Consistently ANZ's strongest performer — record revenue and profit in FY2024.

**Three product lines:**

**Transaction Banking:**
- Cash management
- Trade finance (documentary trade, supply chain finance, commodity financing)
- Payments and clearing
- Deposits

**Loans & Specialised Finance:**
- Loan syndication
- Project and export finance
- Debt structuring and acquisition finance
- Corporate advisory

**Markets:**
- Foreign exchange
- Interest rate risk management
- Debt capital markets
- Commodity finance
- Investor and liability management

**Platform transformation:**
- ANZ Institutional doubled RoE between 2016 and 2024 via scalable internal platform connected to digital revenue-generating services (MIT CISR case study, June 2025)

---

## New Zealand

ANZ Bank New Zealand is New Zealand's largest bank by total assets.

**Products & services:**
- Consumer banking (home loans, deposits, credit cards, digital banking)
- Commercial banking (medium to large enterprises, agribusiness, government)
- ANZ GoMoney app
- KiwiSaver (through ANZ Investments)

**Key metrics:**
- Cash NPAT: A$2,286M (FY2024); A$1,161M (1H25)
- NIM: 2.60% (FY25, up 3bps in 2H25)
- Strong and consistent profit contributor

**CEO:** Antonia Watson (since December 2019)

**Distribution:** Branches, mortgage specialists, relationship managers, digital channels

## Technology & AI Strategy

**Key platforms and initiatives:**

| Initiative | Description | Status |
|---|---|---|
| ANZ Plus | Next-generation digital consumer banking app; rebuilt from scratch | Live/scaling |
| Salesforce Agentforce (AP-first at scale) | AI agents for business bankers — lead surfacing, customer insight visualisation, activity prioritisation | Deployed FY2025 |
| Ensayo AI (with HCL + AWS) | AI for software development lifecycle — coding, testing, deployment automation | Live |
| HCLTech partnership | Digital employee experience transformation across 33 countries | Announced 2025 |
| Multi-cloud (AWS + Azure) | Core banking applications containerised and migrating to cloud | In progress |
| ANZ institutional platform | Scalable digital services platform; doubled RoE 2016–2024 | Mature |

**ICT spend:** ~A$2.5B annually (2024 estimate)

**Cybersecurity posture:**
- Cybersecurity "top technology investment for ANZ CIOs" (Gartner, 2025; 82% of ANZ CIOs rate as key priority)
- "Security must be built into the products and services by design" — ANZ Institutional (October 2025)
- Scope extends to third-party vendors, cloud providers, digital supply chains
- Regulatory cyber accountability now encompasses AI ethics, data transparency, and algorithmic accountability (noted in institutional insights)

**ANZ 2030 Strategy pillars (October 2025):**
1. Customer First
2. Simplicity
3. Resilience
4. Delivering Value

Phase 1 (FY26–FY27): "Get the basics right" — culture reset, Suncorp integration, single customer front-end, productivity, non-financial risk
Phase 2: Invest for growth

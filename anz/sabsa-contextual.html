Contextual Architecture — The Business View

The Contextual Architecture is the top layer of the SABSA framework. It captures the business context — who ANZ is, what it is trying to achieve, what risks it faces, and the environmental constraints it operates within. Everything in the layers below must be traceable back to this layer. This document is derived from ANZ's public annual results, CEO statements, investor presentations, and regulatory filings (2023–2026).

WHAT — Business Assets
Goals · Values · Asset Taxonomy · Success Factors · Targets
Business Goals & Decisions

Execute the ANZ 2030 Strategy — reset culture, complete the Suncorp integration, reduce the cost base by A$800M, and build a platform for long-term growth. Phase 1 (FY26–FY27) is "get the basics right": culture accountability, non-financial risk remediation, Suncorp customer migration, and a single customer front-end. Phase 2 invests that cost savings into growth. The institutional franchise remains the profit engine; Australian retail must recover.

Business Values
  • Customer First — first of the ANZ 2030 pillars; a deliberate culture reset away from internal complexity toward customer outcomes
  • Simplicity — reducing product, process, and organisational complexity; enabling faster decisions and lower cost to serve
  • Resilience — non-financial risk remediation (APRA CEU, ASIC penalty) as a pre-condition for sustainable growth; "security must be built into products and services by design"
  • Delivering Value — returns to shareholders and communities; A$800M cost savings reinvested into the business
  • Accountability and transparency — CEO Nuno Matos explicitly named culture reset and decisiveness as the top priority on appointment
Taxonomy of Business Assets
  • Customer relationships — retail, SME, private banking, and institutional clients across Australia, New Zealand, and major international markets
  • Institutional franchise — consistently ANZ's strongest performer; record revenue and profit in FY2024; RoE doubled 2016–2024; scalable digital platform (MIT CISR case study)
  • Lending book — gross loans grew 13% YoY in FY25 (largely Suncorp); net loans +1% in Q1 FY26; total balance sheet ~A$1T+
  • Suncorp Bank (acquired Jul 2024) — adds ~A$65B in loans, ~A$48B deposits; customer migration underway; full integration by June 2027
  • ANZ Plus platform — next-generation digital consumer banking app, rebuilt from scratch; strategic successor to legacy retail app
  • New Zealand franchise — ANZ Bank NZ is New Zealand's largest bank by assets; Cash NPAT A$2,286M FY24, A$1,161M 1H25; NIM 2.60%
  • Technology infrastructure — multi-cloud (AWS + Azure); Salesforce Agentforce (first at scale in Asia-Pacific); Ensayo AI for software development; HCLTech partnership; ICT spend ~A$2.5B/year
  • Capital and balance sheet — CET1 12.0% (FY25, pro-forma 12.26%); NOHC structure since January 2023; A$1.2B of share buyback completed 1H25
Critical Success Factors
  • Suncorp integration execution — customer migration to ANZ systems by June 2027 is the defining near-term operational risk; delays would extend integration costs and distract management
  • Cost programme delivery — A$800M gross pre-tax annual savings target; 3,500 roles cut + 1,000 managed services exits; cost-to-income ratio below 50% (achieved Q1 FY26)
  • Non-financial risk remediation — APRA CEU compliance and ASIC penalty remediation must be completed without further enforcement escalation
  • Institutional franchise protection — record RoE must be defended; any operational or reputational disruption to the institutional platform would be disproportionately damaging
  • Australian retail recovery — Australia Retail and Business & Private Bank underperformed in FY24–FY25; margin recovery and Suncorp uplift are required for group performance
  • Culture reset credibility — Nuno Matos has staked the ANZ 2030 narrative on accountability and clarity; failure to visibly shift culture would undermine investor confidence
Business Targets
  • Cost: A$800M gross pre-tax annual cost savings from FY26; cost-to-income ratio below 50% (Q1 FY26 achieved)
  • Capital: CET1 target range ~11.5–12.5%; FY25 12.0% (pro-forma 12.26%)
  • NIM: group NIM ~1.56% (FY25); NZ NIM 2.60% (FY25, up 3bps 2H25)
  • Institutional RoE: maintain ~13–14% range established in FY24–FY25
  • Suncorp: full customer migration by June 2027; integration synergies to flow through FY26–FY27
  • Dividend: 166 cps FY25 (83 cps interim + 83 cps final); 70% franked

WHY — Business Motivation
Risk · Opportunities & Threats · Risk Management Strategy
Opportunities & Threats
Opportunities
  • Suncorp acquisition adds scale in Australian retail and business banking — first full year of earnings in FY25
  • Institutional franchise is structurally advantaged — Asia-Pacific trade and capital flows growing; ANZ's institutional platform is a recognised digital leader (MIT CISR)
  • ANZ Plus as future retail growth engine — rebuilt from scratch for digital-native customers; avoids legacy migration cost
  • Salesforce Agentforce (first at scale in Asia-Pacific) — AI productivity gains in business banking could accelerate revenue growth and cost reduction simultaneously
  • Cost savings reinvestment — A$800M savings programme creates capital for strategic investment in FY27+
Threats
  • Regulatory and legal risk — A$250M ASIC penalty (largest ever against an Australian bank) + APRA CEU; ~A$400M combined charge on FY25–FY26 earnings
  • Australian retail margin compression — non-bank and digital competitors continue to pressure home loan margins
  • Suncorp integration risk — customer migration complexity; cultural integration; system failure during migration window
  • Cyber and AI threats — "Generative AI, sophisticated criminal networks, and evolving regulations are reshaping both threats and responses" (ANZ Institutional, October 2025)
  • Culture and talent risk — 3,500 role reductions + managed services exits create execution risk and potential talent drain in critical transformation roles
  • NIM headwinds — competitive mortgage market and RBA rate cycle uncertainty continue to compress Australian retail NIM
Risk Management Strategy
  • Security by design — "Security must be built into the products and services by design" (ANZ Institutional, 2025); cybersecurity is the top technology investment priority for ANZ CIOs (Gartner, 2025)
  • APRA CEU compliance — ~A$150M Root Cause Remediation Plan; multi-year programme to address non-financial risk failures identified in ASIC proceedings
  • Multi-cloud risk diversification — AWS + Azure multi-cloud reduces single-provider dependency for critical banking workloads
  • Third-party and supply chain security — scope explicitly extended to vendors, cloud providers, and digital supply chains
  • Capital buffer — CET1 12.0%+ above APRA's "unquestionably strong" benchmark; NOHC structure provides capital flexibility

HOW — Business Meta-Processes
Business Value Chain · Capabilities · Process Assurance Strategy
Business Value Chain
1
Attract and acquire customers via branch, broker, digital (ANZ Plus app), and institutional relationship coverage across AU, NZ, and international markets
2
Originate and book loans, deposits, FX, and capital markets transactions; credit decisioning supported by AI and ML models
3
Service customers through digital channels (ANZ Plus, ANZ Internet Banking, ANZ GoMoney NZ), contact centres, and relationship managers; Salesforce Agentforce deployed for business bankers
4
Fund the balance sheet via retail and institutional deposits, wholesale markets, and capital raise; manage NIM through repricing and product mix
5
Integrate Suncorp Bank — migrate ~A$65B loan book and ~A$48B deposits onto ANZ systems by June 2027; extract synergies
6
Manage risk and regulatory obligations — APRA CEU remediation, ASIC penalty compliance, CDR obligations, and prudential capital management
Core Capabilities
  • Institutional banking platform — scalable digital services platform for global institutional clients; doubled RoE 2016–2024; connected to revenue-generating digital services
  • ANZ Plus — next-generation consumer digital banking, rebuilt natively; strategic retail growth platform
  • Salesforce Agentforce (AP-first) — AI agents for business bankers; surfaces leads, summarises customer data, prioritises activities; saves ~1 working month/year per banker
  • Ensayo AI (with HCL + AWS) — AI applied across the software development lifecycle: coding, testing, and deployment automation
  • Multi-cloud infrastructure — core banking applications containerised and migrating to AWS + Azure; ~A$2.5B ICT spend/year
  • NZ market leadership — ANZ Bank NZ is largest NZ bank by assets; GoMoney app; KiwiSaver via ANZ Investments; strong and consistent profit contributor

Key Regulators
  • APRA — prudential regulator; Court Enforceable Undertaking (CEU) in force; CET1 "unquestionably strong" benchmark 10.25% for D-SIBs; NOHC capital requirements
  • ASIC — market conduct regulator; A$250M penalty (December 2025, largest ever against an Australian bank)
  • RBNZ — New Zealand prudential regulator; ANZ Bank NZ operates under RBNZ requirements
  • ACCC — competition regulator; Suncorp acquisition approved subject to conditions
  • AUSTRAC — financial intelligence and AML/CTF regulator; ongoing compliance obligations
  • Treasury / CDR framework — open banking data obligations under Consumer Data Right
Trust Framework
  • Shareholders: trust contingent on delivering ANZ 2030 cost programme and NIM stabilisation; halting the A$800M buyback was a signal of strategic prioritisation over short-term returns
  • Regulators: APRA CEU and ASIC penalty represent trust deficit in non-financial risk management; remediation programme is prerequisite for restoring regulatory confidence
  • Institutional clients: trust anchored on ANZ's track record as Asia-Pacific's leading trade and capital markets bank; platform maturity and security posture are differentiators
  • Retail customers: trust built on brand heritage, branch network, and digital transformation (ANZ Plus); Suncorp customers must be migrated without service disruption

# Kiwibank — Business Lines & Processes

**Research date:** 2026-03-18

---

## Overview

Kiwibank is New Zealand's largest NZ-owned bank, fifth-largest overall by assets. Founded 2002, 100% Crown-owned (via Kiwi Group Capital). Over 1 million customers. Market share ~9%. HQ: Wellington.

**Strategic direction:** "Maverick" challenger to the Big Four Australian-owned banks. Growing lending at 1.6–3x market rate. Undergoing full core banking technology transformation.

---

## Retail Banking (Personal)

Core consumer banking products.

**Products & services:**
- Transaction and everyday accounts
- Savings accounts (notice saver, on-call)
- Term deposits
- Home loans (standard variable, fixed, offset, revolving credit)
- Co-own product (up to four applicants, including friends/whanau — innovative for NZ)
- Personal loans
- Credit cards
- Foreign exchange
- Digital banking (Kiwibank app, internet banking)

**Key processes:**
- Digital-first onboarding and servicing
- Confirmation of Payee (anti-scam, deployed among first NZ banks)
- Open banking APIs (fees removed; open banking ready June–December 2026 under CDR)
- Physical branch network — largest in NZ; ongoing investment (e.g., Newmarket, Auckland)

**Home loan market:** ~8% of total NZ home loan stock; capturing ~25% of new originations (per ANZ analysis, 2024); growing 1.6–2.7x market rate.

---

## Business Banking

Growing challenger in NZ business lending.

**Products & services:**
- Business transaction accounts
- SME lending (term loans, overdrafts, revolving credit)
- Sustainable business loans (preferential rates for environmental outcomes)
- Commercial property lending
- Asset and equipment finance
- Business insurance (through partners)

**Key metrics:**
- Business lending: NZD $8.7B (December 2025)
- Business lending grew 3x+ faster than market in FY2024
- Growing $400M per half-year in recent periods

**Key processes:**
- Faster lending decisions for small businesses
- nCino platform for retail and business banking loan origination (part of tech transformation)
- First end-to-end business loan processed on new Thought Machine core banking platform (April 2025 — major milestone)

---

## KiwiSaver and Insurance (Historical)

**Exited in 2022.** Kiwibank sold Kiwi Wealth (KiwiSaver and investment management) in 2022 to refocus on core banking. KiwiSaver is now managed by Fisher Funds Management Limited under the Kiwibank brand.

The sale proceeds funded a NZD $225M capital injection into the bank (July 2023), strengthening the balance sheet for growth.

---

## Core Banking Technology Transformation

Kiwibank is mid-way through a full technology stack replacement. This is the defining operational initiative of the organisation.

**Architecture approach:** "Run-and-replace" — build new cloud-native stack alongside legacy systems; over half the platform being remediated or replaced simultaneously.

| Component | Technology | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Core banking platform | Thought Machine Vault (cloud-native; same as Macquarie Bank AU) | First business loan processed April 2025 |
| Cloud infrastructure | AWS (primary) | In progress |
| CRM | Microsoft Dynamics | Deployed |
| Retail/business lending origination | nCino | Deployed |
| Data platform | Snowflake + Apache Kafka | Deployed |
| Product catalog | Zafin (deployed October 2025) | Live |
| Contact centre / AI voicebot | Genesys Cloud | Live — 98% reduction in intents handled by live agents |

**Outcomes to date:**
- Sixfold improvement in service downtime
- 70% of customers use the Kiwibank app more than once a day
- 28% reduction in call abandonment (Genesys voicebot)
- 19% reduction in average handling time
- 27% decrease in transfer rates
- NZ CIO of the Year 2024 (Hamish Rumbold, CDTO)
- "Second-best reliability and availability of any bank in the Southern Hemisphere" (self-reported)

**McKinsey case study:** Recognised as a transformation model — cross-functional squads of engineers, architects, data scientists, and product owners; "people-first" change management.

## AI Strategy

**Approach:** "People first" — AI to reduce repetitive tasks and free staff for customer-facing work. CEO Steve Jurkovich: using AI to "bring a bit more humanity back into banking."

**Current deployments:**
- Genesys AI voicebot (contact centre automation)
- AI-powered personalised in-app messaging (Atomic.io)
- Internal AI tools to reduce admin burden on staff

**Cybersecurity and fraud:**
- "Secure by design" and "compliant by design" foundational principles
- Enhanced fraud protection delivered in FY25
- Among first NZ banks to deploy Confirmation of Payee
- Open banking API fees removed (only NZ bank to do so proactively)

## Open Banking and Regulation

| Requirement | Deadline | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Designated data holder | 1 June 2026 | Under Customer Data Right |
| Payment APIs open banking ready | June 2026 | Major banks required December 2025 |
| Broader open banking services | December 2026 | — |

Kiwibank proactively removed open banking API fees ahead of regulatory requirements — the only NZ bank to do so.

## Executive Statements

**Steve Jurkovich (CEO):**

- On growth: "We've doubled our size in five years and aim to do so again." (~10% annual growth without "edgy and risky" risk-taking)
- On technology: "Building a modern, resilient bank that can offer new and competitive products faster"
- On AI: "People first" — focus on using AI to reduce repetitive tasks and augment staff, not replace them; "bring a bit more humanity back into banking"
- On IPO: Advocates waiting until tech transformation is substantially complete (estimated 18 months–2 years further de-risking needed)
- On capital and ownership: Open to some foreign investment; government should retain a majority stake
- On economy (H1 FY26): "Lending and deposit growth continuing to outperform the market and business confidence expected to lift... economic activity is forecast to broaden through 2026"

**Context — Commerce Commission (2024):**
Commerce Commission 14-month market study found NZ personal banking is "a stable, highly profitable, two-tier oligopoly." Recommended government provide Kiwibank more capital to make it "a disruptive competitor." Government accepted all 14 recommendations — directly led to the (ultimately scrapped) $500M capital raise process.

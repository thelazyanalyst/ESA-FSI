Contextual Architecture — The Business View

The Contextual Architecture is the top layer of the SABSA framework. It captures the business context — who Kiwibank is, what it is trying to achieve, what risks it faces, and the environmental constraints it operates within. Everything in the layers below must be traceable back to this layer. This document is derived from Kiwibank's public press releases, annual results, CEO statements, and Commerce Commission proceedings (2022–2026).

WHAT — Business Assets
Goals · Business Value · Business Attributes Taxonomy & Profile
Business Goals & Decisions

Become a genuine, scaled competitor to the four Australian-owned banks. Double in size every five years. Achieve 25%+ of new NZ home lending originations and grow business lending at 3× market rate — without taking "edgy and risky" risks. Execute a full core banking transformation to deliver lasting product agility.

Business Value
  • 1M+ customers across retail and business banking; NZD $37.6B total lending book (Dec 2025)
  • NZ-owned brand — Crown mandate as the designated competitive challenger; "Maverick" positioning is a strategic asset
  • Core banking transformation platform (Thought Machine, Zafin, nCino, AWS) — future product agility and cost competitiveness
  • Branch network — largest in NZ; physical presence as a trust, accessibility, and distribution differentiator
  • Regulatory goodwill — proactive Commerce Commission engagement; only NZ bank to remove open banking API fees

WHY — Business Motivation
Risk · Opportunities & Threats · Risk Management Strategy
Opportunities & Threats
Opportunities
  • Commerce Commission mandate to be a "disruptive competitor" — regulatory tailwind for capital, market access, and open banking
  • RBNZ capital requirement easing (~NZD $500M benefit) — balance sheet capacity without dilutive equity raise
  • Australian banks' legacy infrastructure creates a product speed advantage post-transformation
  • Open banking CDR framework enables fintech partnerships and new revenue streams
  • NZ economic recovery in 2026 — business confidence lifting; credit impairments falling sharply (H1 FY26: $5M vs $21M prior)
Threats
  • NIM compression (down 11bps in H1 FY26) — volume growth required to sustain profitability
  • Core transformation execution risk — "run-and-replace"; 50%+ of tech stack in simultaneous remediation
  • Australian banks matching open banking moves — competitive advantage window is finite
  • APP fraud and scams — reputational and financial risk; Code of Banking Practice obligations from 30 Nov 2025
  • Cyber threats to cloud-native transformation infrastructure (AWS, Thought Machine, Snowflake)
  • Governance constraints — Crown ownership limits capital raise flexibility and IPO timing
Risk Management Strategy
  • "Secure by design, compliant by design" — foundational engineering principles applied across the transformation programme
  • Transform the technology base to reduce operational and execution risk; cloud-native reduces legacy fragility
  • Proactive regulatory engagement — accepted all 14 Commerce Commission recommendations before mandated
  • Capital buffer maintained well above RBNZ minimums (15.3% total capital vs 4.5% CET1 floor)
  • Confirmation of Payee deployed among first NZ banks — reduces APP fraud liability ahead of regulatory deadline

WHERE — Business Geography
Operating Jurisdiction · Regulatory Domains · Infrastructure Geography
Operating Jurisdiction
  • New Zealand only — no international retail or commercial banking operations; NZ-focused capital raise (NZ institutional investors only)
  • HQ: Wellington; Branch network: nationwide — largest in NZ; recent expansion includes Newmarket (Auckland) and Kerikeri
  • ~9% overall NZ banking market share; ~8% home loan stock; capturing ~25% of new originations (2024)
Regulatory Domains
  • Reserve Bank of New Zealand (RBNZ) — prudential regulation; standardised capital approach (not IRB like the Big Four)
  • Commerce Commission — competition regulation; outcome of Banking Competition Inquiry directly shapes Kiwibank's strategic positioning
  • Consumer Data Right (CDR) framework — open banking regulatory domain; Kiwibank as designated data holder
  • NZ Code of Banking Practice — scam/fraud obligations; effective 30 November 2025
Infrastructure Geography
  • Cloud-primary: AWS as primary cloud provider for core banking and data workloads
  • Payment processing: ACI Worldwide cloud-hosted managed service — first NZ bank to shift payment processing to cloud; SaaS deployment on Microsoft Azure
  • Data: Snowflake + Apache Kafka (real-time data streaming); centralised data platform
  • No significant on-premise datacentre footprint remaining post-transformation

Operational Time Dependencies
